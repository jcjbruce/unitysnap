03 · Scope, Approach & Deliverables

Everything the RFP requires. And more.

Our scope addresses every requirement in the BCFC RFP — from discovery and strategy through to post-launch support — while bringing forward-thinking design that reflects Boulder County's cinematic identity.

Phase 01
Discovery & Strategy
  • Stakeholder interviews with BCFC and Chamber team
  • UX audit — performance, structure, and content gaps
  • New information architecture and revised sitemap
  • User personas and journey mapping (filmmakers, producers, scouts)
  • Creative brief and mood board development
Phase 02
Design
  • 2–3 creative directions with mood boards for BCFC review
  • Full responsive design — desktop, tablet, and mobile
  • Visual style guide: typography, colour, photography direction
  • Interactive prototypes for key page templates
  • Location gallery design and interactive map approach
Phase 03
Development
  • WordPress CMS setup (Kadence visual builder)
  • Fully responsive, mobile-first build across all devices
  • Open-source fonts and resources prioritized throughout
  • Video and hi-res image galleries
  • Interactive location gallery with map integration
  • Creative Directory — searchable and filterable
  • Blog, newsletter, and user walkthrough web tools
  • All plugins sustainable with limited ongoing costs
  • WCAG 2.1 AA compliance throughout
Phases 04–06
Content, QA & Launch
  • Full content migration from current site
  • Optional content rewriting and restructuring
  • SEO setup — sitemap, metadata, Core Web Vitals
  • Cross-device and cross-browser QA
  • Accessibility validation and final audit
  • CMS training for BCFC and Chamber staff
  • 30-day post-launch support and bug-fix warranty
Technical Approach & CMS Recommendation

WordPress + Kadence — Built for Sustainability

We recommend WordPress as the CMS for BCFC — the most widely adopted open-source CMS in the world, powering over 40% of all websites. Paired with the Kadence visual builder, this gives BCFC staff full control over content, layout, and media without developer dependency.

Open-source foundation — no licensing fees, no vendor lock-in, massive ecosystem of free plugins and themes
Kadence visual builder — intuitive drag-and-drop editing that non-technical staff can learn in a single training session
Sustainable plugin strategy — all plugins selected for long-term viability, active maintenance, and zero or minimal recurring costs
Performance-first architecture — optimized hosting, CDN delivery, lazy loading, and Core Web Vitals tuning for sub-2-second load times
WCAG 2.1 AA from the ground up — semantic HTML, ARIA landmarks, keyboard navigation, color contrast validation, and screen reader testing
Open-source typography — all fonts sourced from Google Fonts or similar free libraries, ensuring zero ongoing font licensing costs
