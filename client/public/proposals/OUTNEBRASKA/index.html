02 — Technical Approach

Built right, from the
ground up.

Every technical decision we make is driven by two questions: will this serve OutNebraska's constituents well, and will your staff be able to manage it independently after we're done? Here's how we approach each piece of the build.

Page Builder: Kadence Pro

We recommend Kadence Pro over Elementor. The reason is straightforward: Kadence produces a lighter codebase, faster load times, and cleaner semantic HTML — all of which matter for accessibility and performance. Its block-based editing model is more intuitive for non-developers, and it's built for long-term maintainability. Your staff will be able to manage the site independently after training. That's the point.

Recommended over Elementor

Bill Tracker

We'll keep ACF as the data backbone — your existing ACF structure is a solid reference point and we'll evaluate it during discovery. The front end gets a complete rebuild: a filterable, sortable display using ACF + custom WP_Query with lightweight JavaScript filtering. Clean table or card view. Staff can update it in under two minutes. Constituents can scan it in under ten seconds. That's the goal.

ACF + Custom WP_Query

EveryAction Integration

Phase 1: Embed EveryAction forms styled to feel fully native to the site — not obviously embedded. Phase 2: Explore automating data flow via Zapier or direct EveryAction REST API webhooks so form submissions push directly into the CRM without manual export. Newsletter signup connected to EveryAction list. Event registration via EveryAction where applicable. A tasteful, non-intrusive email capture pop-up will be implemented for newsletter signup using MailOptin or a lightweight custom solution — triggered by scroll depth or exit intent, dismissible with one click, and fully keyboard-accessible.

Native-Feel Embeds + API Automation

Accessibility: WCAG 2.1 AA

Accessibility is built in from day one — not audited at the end. Color contrast checked during design. Semantic HTML throughout. Keyboard navigation tested at every milestone. Skip links, ARIA labels, proper heading hierarchy, and alt text standards documented in the staff handoff guide. We use axe DevTools during development. Accessibility is a design requirement, not a compliance checkbox.

WCAG 2.1 AA — Built In, Not Bolted On

Performance

Kadence Pro is lightweight by design. We'll coordinate with your existing Cloudflare setup — not override it. Image optimization via ShortPixel. Lazy loading enabled. Minification and caching via LiteSpeed Cache (SiteGround-native) or WP Rocket. Target: page load under 3 seconds on mobile. We test with GTmetrix and Google PageSpeed Insights before launch.

Target: <3s Mobile Load

Event Calendar

The Events Calendar Pro with Community Events add-on for public submission and staff approval workflow. Both list view and calendar view included. "Add to calendar" (.ics / Google Calendar) built in. EveryAction event registration embedded or linked per event. Staff can manage the approval queue without touching code.

Events Calendar Pro + Community Events

Resource Directory

Searchable and filterable by category, region (local/national/international), and Nebraska geography. ACF-driven structure. Fast, intuitive browsing — minimal clicking to find what you need. Built to be updated by staff without developer involvement. The directory is a lifeline for many of your constituents; it needs to work flawlessly.

ACF-Driven + Staff-Managed

Donation Page

The Donation Page is a high-complexity, high-stakes page — it's a direct revenue driver and a moment of trust. The EveryAction donation form will be embedded and styled natively to the site, not as an obvious third-party widget. We'll work with OutNebraska's EveryAction configuration to ensure the form matches the site's visual identity and is fully accessible. Suggested giving amounts, recurring donation options, and mobile optimization are all included.

EveryAction — Native Embed

Legislative Session Pages

Campaign-specific pages built to be activated, updated, and archived across legislative sessions without developer involvement. Staff can update bill status, add action alerts, and publish session summaries directly from the WordPress back end. These pages are designed to be fast to update during active sessions and easy to archive when a session ends.

Staff-Managed — Session-Ready

Contact Page

A clean, accessible contact page with a custom form built in Gravity Forms — the most widely supported form plugin in the nonprofit WordPress ecosystem, with deep EveryAction compatibility and a familiar editing interface for staff. Form submissions routed to the appropriate staff inbox. Spam protection via honeypot and reCAPTCHA. Fully accessible and mobile-optimized.

Gravity Forms — Recommended

Content Migration

Full export/import with URL mapping. 301 redirects managed via the Redirection plugin — no broken links, no lost SEO equity. All images run through optimization during migration. Existing SEO metadata preserved and improved using Yoast. Blog posts and press statements are treated as separate content types with distinct templates and archives, migrated completely. We document every redirect for your records.

Zero Broken Links — Full SEO Preservation

03 — Project Timeline

Eight milestones.
No surprises.

Every project we deliver follows a structured, milestone-based process with clear checkpoints and documented deliverables at every stage. Here's how we get from kickoff to launch for OutNebraska.

Discovery & Architecture Weeks 1–2 · April 1–14, 2026

Stakeholder calls, content inventory review, sitemap and information architecture finalization, ACF field planning, and plugin stack confirmation. What we need from OutNebraska: access to the current site, analytics data, brand guidelines, and EveryAction account details.

Design in Figma Weeks 3–5 · April 15 – May 5, 2026

Full design comps for all high-complexity pages. Two rounds of revisions included. Sign-off required before development begins. You approve every major decision before a line of code is written.

Development on Staging Weeks 6–13 · May 6 – June 30, 2026

WordPress build on SiteGround staging environment. Custom post types, ACF fields, EveryAction integrations, Bill Tracker, Resource Directory, Event Calendar, Donation Page (native EveryAction embed), Legislative Session Pages, Contact Page (Gravity Forms), and all page templates. Weekly progress updates throughout development.

Content Migration & Population Weeks 14–15 · July 1–14, 2026

Migration of existing content, press statements, and news posts. 301 redirects configured. Image optimization applied during migration. URL preservation verified and documented.

QA, Accessibility Audit & Performance Testing Week 16 · July 15–21, 2026

Full cross-browser and cross-device QA. axe DevTools accessibility audit. GTmetrix and PageSpeed Insights testing. All issues resolved before handoff — nothing ships with known problems.

Staff Training Week 17 · July 22–28, 2026

Two to three sessions with two to three staff members. Covers back-end management, content editing, event calendar, bill tracker, and site settings. Written documentation and video walkthroughs delivered — so your team has a reference long after training ends.

Launch Target: Mid–Late Q3 2026 (Q4 2026 if needed)

DNS cutover coordinated with SiteGround and Cloudflare. 30-day stabilization period included. We stay close through go-live and the weeks that follow.

Post-Launch Support 90 Days Post-Launch

Developer remains available for questions, fixes, and minor adjustments. You're not on your own the moment the site goes live.

05 — How We Work

Transparent process.
Real partnership.

We believe the best client relationships are built on clear communication, honest expectations, and consistent follow-through. Here's what working with Civic Firm actually looks like.

Who Does the Work

Work is performed by Civic Firm directly. The design phase may involve a trusted collaborator; any subcontractors will be disclosed and vetted before engagement. You'll always know who's working on your project.

Communication

Weekly check-ins via video call or async update. Slack or email preferred. 24-hour response SLA on business days. We don't disappear between milestones — you'll always know where things stand.

Availability & Time Zone

Remote-first, North America. Available during standard business hours (Central Time) with flexibility for stakeholder calls across time zones. Emergency post-launch support available outside business hours.

Payment Schedule

33% at kickoff / 33% at midpoint milestone / 33% at launch — confirmed and accepted as proposed in the RFP. No surprise invoices. Milestone-based billing keeps everyone aligned.

Scope Changes

Any work outside the agreed scope requires a written change order before it begins — no surprises on either side. Out-of-scope work is billed at an agreed hourly rate of $95/hr. If anything emerges during development that could affect timeline or budget, we flag it immediately and collaboratively decide how to proceed before moving forward.

Feedback & Revision Cycles

During development, you review the site on a live staging URL at each milestone checkpoint. Feedback is collected async via a shared document or Loom video — no need for a live call unless you prefer it. Two rounds of revisions are included per milestone. Additional revision rounds are available at the agreed hourly rate. Turnaround on revisions is typically 3–5 business days.

Post-Launch Stability

90-day post-launch support period included in all options. Developer remains available for questions, fixes, and minor adjustments. We're not done when the site goes live — we're done when you're confident.

Platform Constraints

WordPress on SiteGround — confirmed and non-negotiable, as specified in the RFP. We work within your existing infrastructure, not around it. Cloudflare configuration will be coordinated, not overridden.
